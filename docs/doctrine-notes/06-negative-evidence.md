# Negative Evidence

## Purpose

This note establishes the minimum doctrine for interpreting absence, silence, and resumed observation.

## Current doctrinal rule

Negative evidence is interpretive, not automatic.

Non-observation does not equal breakage. It matters only relative to expectations, basis, and contradictory evidence.

## Minimum classes of absence

For current doctrine, absence can be treated in three broad ways:

- `neutral absence`: no observation occurred, but nothing about the situation made observation strongly expected
- `weak negative evidence`: observation or persistence was somewhat expected, but the miss is not decisive
- `strong negative evidence`: observed conditions or contradictions make continued tracking substantially less plausible

## What should inform the interpretation

- inertia expectations for the referent
- volatility expectations for the surrounding conditions
- observer basis limits
- missed expected encounters
- contradictory triggers or happenings

## What this commits the repo to

- absence is basis-relative
- negative evidence can accumulate without becoming absolute proof
- resumed observation does not prove uninterrupted continuity by itself

## What remains open

The repository does not yet claim:

- a fixed accumulation function for absence
- a single universal negative evidence score
- identical expectations across all observers or referents

## Working implementation implication

For labs and code:

- represent absence interpretation separately from raw lack of events
- allow continuity estimates to weaken without immediately breaking
- keep contradictory evidence distinct from silence
