# Emergence, Seed, Seat, Materialization

## Purpose

This note establishes the current minimum doctrine for how observers and non-observers emerge without magical appearance.

It also sets the current relation between:

- `Seed`
- `Seat`
- `Adoption`
- `Cultivation`
- `Materialization`
- `Custody`

## Current doctrinal rule

Entities should not simply appear without any emergence path.

Normal emergence should be representable through precursor structure, inheritance, cultivation, observation, or adoption.

Direct injection into already-lived continuity is exceptional, not the normal explanation for appearance.

## Seed

A `Seed` is a precursor of continuity or realization.

It is structured potential rather than full materialization.

A seed may support later emergence of:

- a non-observer referent
- an observer seat
- a successor continuity
- a dormant or partial candidate entity

The current doctrine does not require every seed to materialize.

## Seat

A `Seat` is a special seed or prepared locus for observer continuity.

A seat differs from a generic seed because it is shaped for occupation or adoption by an observer.

Current doctrine should treat seats as:

- situated
- occupancy-bearing
- continuity-relevant
- optionally custody-bearing

Seats make it possible to explain observer emergence without requiring arbitrary branch injection.

## Adoption

`Adoption` means an observer takes up an already-prepared continuity surface, such as a seat or custodial branch.

Adoption does not mean retroactive authorship of all prior continuity.

The current doctrine should allow:

- inherited continuity
- forked continuity after adoption
- carried-forward nuclei
- local continuation after occupancy

## Cultivation

`Cultivation` means existing continuity prepares or maintains conditions for later emergence.

Examples include:

- preparing seeds
- maintaining seats
- preserving custody-bearing continuity for later adoption

Cultivation is useful because it explains how emergence can be prepared without requiring immediate realization.

## Materialization

`Materialization` is when a candidate continuity becomes sufficiently realized in tracked structure to function as a stable entity in branch history.

Current doctrine should treat materialization as:

- an emergence process
- basis-sensitive
- observer-mediated
- not identical to universal agreement

For non-observer referents, materialization may be pressured by multiple observer histories, but it should not be defined as consensus itself.

## Custody

`Custody` is the local authority or stewardship relation over a continuity surface, seat, or emergence path.

Custody is relevant when:

- a seat is maintained for later occupation
- an emergence surface has admissibility rules
- externally authored contribution would otherwise blur continuity

Custody should remain local and contextual, not substrate-universal.

## Observer and non-observer emergence

The current doctrine should distinguish:

- observer emergence
- non-observer emergence

They are related, but not identical.

Observer emergence is more naturally explained by:

- seats
- adoption
- inheritance
- forked birth
- cultivated continuity

Non-observer emergence is more naturally explained by:

- seeds
- observation pressure
- tracked realization
- lineage or precursor continuity

## What this commits the repo to

- normal emergence should not require injection
- seeds and seats are related but not identical
- observer and non-observer emergence are related but not identical
- materialization is an emergence process, not magical appearance
- custody may matter locally without becoming a universal truth authority

## What remains open

The repository does not yet claim:

- a final seed schema
- a final seat schema
- a universal materialization threshold
- a final custody model
- final rules for when multiple observer histories yield one stable materialized referent

## Working implementation implication

For current doctrine and labs:

- prefer emergence through precursor structure over arbitrary insertion
- represent seeds and seats explicitly when they are doing explanatory work
- treat direct injection as exceptional
- allow non-observer referents to be pressured by many observers without forcing consensus into the base ontology
