# Ontology

## Core purpose

`causal-substrate` models how causality becomes realized, tracked, persisted, and related across observers, referents, contexts, and projections.

It should support:

- observer-relative branch continuity
- referent persistence and tracking
- context containment
- portal or membrane exposure across contexts
- wake/sleep segmentation of history
- lineage and nucleus inheritance
- salience and threshold-based awareness
- optional comparison, convergence, and agreement-like behaviors
- local-first and decentralized-friendly operation

## Posture

There is no guaranteed global truth.

Reality may be observer-relative, branch-relative, basis-relative, partial, and only weakly shared. Agreement, convergence, and consensus are optional higher-order behaviors, not substrate primitives.

This repository is therefore:

- observer-first
- local-first
- decentralization-friendly
- ontology-preserving before optimization-heavy

## Load-bearing concepts

### Observer

A locus that preserves distinctions across change and can be affected by them.

### Basis

The distinctions a branch or observer can preserve or compare. Basis is explicit, but may be partial, compositional, degraded, projected, or revised.

### Trigger

A threshold crossing where sensed or inferred change becomes significant enough to register.

### Salience

Observer-specific value or importance weighting that determines which triggers matter enough to shape branch state or strengthen tracking.

### Branch

A continuity of preserved distinctions over time. A branch is not treated as a mere timeline; it can support compositional or higher-dimensional structure.

### Happening

An important clustered change or keyframe within a branch segment. A happening can summarize many lower-level triggers.

### Wake / Sleep

A wake period is an active segment between sleep points. Sleep seals a segment into colder storage or a durable chunk and yields a carry-forward inheritance package.

### Nucleus

A carried-forward ancestry or core anchor inherited from prior history or prior referents. It is not just a pointer to the last chunk.

### Referent

A persistent cause or tracked continuity. Referents can have branches of their own and are tracked by observers rather than naively created by them.

### Binding

A relationship between observer branches and referent branches.

### Inertia

A policy or model for predicted continuity of a referent under absence of observation.

### Volatility

Expected environmental change affecting how absence and persistence should be interpreted.

### Lineage

Ancestry relations between referents or segments, including continuation, split, merge, and seed-origin.

### Context

A branch with containment semantics. Observers and referents are situated within contexts.

### Portal

A seeded membrane or proxy branch exposing a constrained transform of an enclosing, adjacent, or external context into a local context.

### View

A derived representation over branches, referents, contexts, or segments. Views are replaceable; preserved branch and segment history is not.

## Distinctions that must not collapse

- trigger != salience
- branch != referent
- context != ordinary referent
- portal != generic child object
- view != source truth
- convergence != branch
- agreement != substrate primitive
- identity != lineage
- equivalence != compatibility
- compatibility != convergence

## Current repository rule

When uncertain, preserve optionality and avoid narrowing the ontology too early.
